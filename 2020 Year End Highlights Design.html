2020 - Year End Highlights
What a year it has been! There are so many testimonies of God's Faithful Hand and His Abundant Provision!

We want to remember all the wonderful moments that happened this year. So often in scripture, we are reminded to recall and return in gratitude for all that God has done. Stirring up faith, and acknowledging God's Power and Providence in what we've put our hands to. We are amazed at seeing His Hand faithfully guiding us into things we hadn’t even known were possibilities.

The year started in Cape Town, South Africa. While visiting a dear pastor friend, living and working in a local township, we learned of a fire that had swept through the neighborhood destroying 100 shanty homes on New Year’s Day. Walking through the burn area it was clear something had to be done for several of the elderly ladies that had not received aid to rebuild. Some young people from the UK and the USA stepped in and we ending up providing materials to build 4.

Another sweet moment unfolded as we visited a church in a different township area. They currently host an after school tutoring and soccer program that includes a devotional time with the kids. As a result of the kindness shown to the kids during this time, they often do not want to go back home (because of chaos and alcohol problems common in this area) and many sleep on the floor inside the church. The pastor's wife expressed interest in creating a vegetable garden to help feed the kids and teach them how to grow things. Specific gifts and donations were given to make this a reality.
With lots of new young believers, they needed their own bibles. We purchased bibles for 50 kids!
A friend kindly donated some pots and pans to some families affected by the fire.

We would be remiss if we didn’t include a highlight from this year when we had the opportunity to participate in a program run by the Message Trust ZA at the prison Nelson Mandela was freed from! Mark and Faith had not visited a high-security prison before. The group we worked with were entirely young people. We gathered informally in a circle on benches that surrounded a small room and spoke openly about what God was doing in our lives, encouraging one another. There was such an anointing on the songs we sang together and the prayers led by the inmates. It was a special moment that we feel privileged to have been a part of. Unfortunately, we could not take pictures inside the prison.

Though covid eventually squashed larger gatherings, we initially squeezed in a few!

A fully funded goat project in Ethiopia! This will serve pioneering missionaries in their work, providing some income for their families.

Praying with those we meet along the way.

We are helping supply bibles and facilitating training for local leaders to start bible studies in unreached villages in the Philippines in partnership with CMC friends.

Hannah in Vietnam continues to work with school children and has found a wonderful spot across the street from a very large school! Now the kids come to her to study English and the Bible. She Recently had her leg operated on to remove a pin that have been installed as a result of a motorcycle accident. It was causing her great pain and we were able to help with the hospital fees.

Pastor Vuyo, in South Africa, organized a team of local volunteers to cook, set up, and distribute food in 6 different locations through the 8 months Covid had them on lockdown. Each outreach fed about 180+ people. At the busiest time, they had three feedings a week! Thousands were fed.
 

Praise the Lord!

Rubber boats were distributed to those without winter shoes. School shoes will be distributed soon so more kids will be able to attend school (uniforms are required) now that it is re-opened.
     

Sharing a word to those in line for soup kitchen.


Thank you to all who have given to make this happen!
We so appreciate your prayers and support.
